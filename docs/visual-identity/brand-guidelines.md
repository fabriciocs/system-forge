# Brand Guidelines

## Brand Essence

System Forge is a governed system-factory platform. The identity should feel editorial, precise, and infrastructural: more like a control plane than a consumer app.

## Symbol Concepts Considered

### 1. Layered Node Frame

- A square frame with internal nodes and connectors.
- References scope, orchestration, and versioned structure.
- Strong in small sizes and works in monochrome.

### 2. Timeline Spine

- A vertical spine with checkpoints.
- Communicates staged execution and progression.
- Less distinctive as a primary mark.

### 3. Document Stack Monogram

- Overlapping sheets or pages.
- Good link to documentation, but too literal and less ownable.

## Chosen Concept

Layered Node Frame.

Why:

- It maps directly to the product idea: layered governance, stages, outputs, and controlled execution.
- It remains recognizable at 16x16 and 32x32.
- It can render as a simple monochrome mark without losing meaning.
- It can scale into a wordmark, favicon, and product icon set.

## Construction

- Grid: 24 x 24 unit system.
- Base geometry: outer square frame, inner inset frame, four corner anchors, and a central node.
- Stroke logic: consistent line weight with rounded joins and caps.
- Balance: equal visual weight on all sides to support left-to-right and centered usage.

## Proportions

- Outer frame occupies most of the canvas with safe breathing room.
- Inner frame is inset to create a layered control-plane effect.
- Center node should remain visually dominant in small sizes.
- Corner anchors should never touch the edge.

## Clear Space

- Minimum clear space equals the size of the central node radius on each side.
- No text, border, or adjacent icon should enter the protected area.

## Minimum Sizes

- Symbol only: 16 px minimum.
- Primary logo lockup: 120 px minimum width.
- Horizontal logo: 160 px minimum width.
- Favicon: 16 px, with the symbol simplified and centered.

## Color Palette

### Core

- Ink: `#111827`
- Slate: `#334155`
- Surface: `#F3F4F6`
- Bone: `#E5E7EB`

### Accent

- Signal Blue: `#2563EB`
- Signal Amber: `#D97706`

### Negative / Dark

- Night: `#0B1220`
- Soft White: `#F8FAFC`

## Typography

- Preferred display style: condensed sans for titles and labels.
- Preferred body style: neutral sans with high legibility.
- Tone: technical, editorial, and restrained.
- Avoid decorative serif display and over-rounded geometric typefaces.

## Allowed Backgrounds

- Light neutral surfaces.
- Dark technical surfaces.
- Solid monochrome backgrounds.
- Controlled editorial gradients only if they do not reduce contrast.

## Incorrect Uses

- Do not stretch or skew the mark.
- Do not add drop shadows to the symbol.
- Do not place the symbol inside a noisy decorative container.
- Do not recolor individual segments independently.
- Do not rotate the symbol as a decorative motif.
- Do not use low-contrast combinations that weaken the frame or center node.

## Co-branding

- System Forge should keep the symbol left-aligned with text when paired with partner marks.
- Preserve equal visual weight and avoid crowding adjacent logos.
- In lockups, the symbol should remain the strongest identifier.

## Visual Rules

- Prefer tight structure over ornament.
- Use straight lines, measured spacing, and deliberate corners.
- Keep the icon simple enough to survive on small surfaces.
- Maintain consistency between product icon, favicon, and marketing usage.

## Risk Notes

- If the frame becomes too detailed, small-size recognition will drop.
- If the palette becomes too colorful, the enterprise tone weakens.
- If the wordmark becomes overly condensed, accessibility and readability suffer.

## Hypotheses

- A layered control-frame symbol will read as governance and orchestration rather than a generic app badge.
- The mark will remain usable in monochrome contexts such as invoices, documents, and browser tabs.
- The visual system will feel distinct if the geometry stays precise and the palette stays restrained.
